Tips and Examples of Product Photography


Good vs Bad Lighting

This may seem obvious, but it is an important component of a professional and effective product photo to make sure that the product is shot in good lighting. As you can see in the example above the lighting on the left is much worse than the example above to the right. You can see the glare of the product on the backdrop causing it to look messy and unprofessional, which doesn't make the product look very good.


Rule of Thirds

The rule of thirds is a method that allows you to produce stunning and natural photos while still showcasing the subject of the photo. To achieve this you must think, or divide, the area by two horizontal and two vertical lines and try and place the subject of the photograph on the intersection of these lines in order to make a natural photo.


Photography Angles

Finally, in order to make sure that you get the best possible photo of the product it is always good practice to try out a variety of different camera angles. Different angles will work better for different products, it depends on the material of the product the lighting and the location, as well as many other factors. So it is best to try out the angles shown above to try and get the best visual that you can.
